## Quaestio XCVI — *De Ultimo Viro Stante*
### (On the Last Man Standing)

---

### Articulus I — *Utrum Hugo recte elegerit Angliam?*
**Whether Huw rightly chose England.**

**Videtur quod non.** It seems that he did not.

**Objectio 1.** The safer pick is ever to be preferred. But Spain stands at 74.6%,
and England at but 66.2%. Therefore Huw chose the worse, and by 8.4 parts in a
hundred.

**Praeterea, Objectio 2.** *Consensus gentium signum veritatis* — the agreement of
many is a sign of truth. But five chose Spain, and but one England. Therefore the
five have truth, and the one has folly.

**Praeterea, Objectio 3.** England is walled in by Argentina in the semi-final, a
coin's toss at 48.9%. It is unfitting to spend a great team upon a small round, and
unfitting to hold one that cannot go deep.

**Sed contra,** it is written in the Rules of the Game: *"The game continues until
there is one player left standing, who is declared the winner."* But he who stands
with the many does not stand *last*; he stands *among*. Therefore to stand alone is
the whole end of the thing.

**Respondeo dicendum quod** a pick possesses two distinct powers: the *virtus
conservandi*, by which it preserves him who makes it, and the *virtus dividendi*, by
which it divides him from his fellows. The vulgar consider only the first.

Now it is written further: *"If all Active Players choose a team that Loses, all
remaining players continue."* Whence it follows — and let the reader mark this well
— that **a unanimous field is immortal.** Had all six taken Spain, Belgium could have
slain precisely nobody. Death could not enter the garden, for there was no man
outside it.

Therefore Huw, choosing England, did not merely *avoid* the peril of the herd.
**He created it.** By standing apart he made it false that all had chosen losers,
and thereby armed a weapon which, until he touched it, was inert. He did not flee
their correlation; he took it up and turned it against them.

Moreover, having spent England, he alone retains **both France and Spain** — that is,
both sides of the semi-final of the first half — and therefore cannot be stranded
upon that side, whichever prevails. The other five, having spent Spain, hold France
alone, and are as men who have staked all upon a single horse whilst claiming the
virtue of prudence.

**Ad primum ergo dicendum quod** the objection reckons the price and forgets the
purchase. Those 8.4 parts buy a **16.8% chance of the entire pot, at once, in the
quarter-finals.** And the price is anyway overstated: by the all-lose rule his true
peril is **25.2%, not 33.8%**, for whenever Spain also falls he is redeemed. He
survives at 74.8%; the herd at 83.2%. He has bought the pot for eight points and
change.

**Ad secundum dicendum quod** the consensus of the many is a sign of truth in matters
of *fact*, but in matters of a *divided pot* it is a sign only of dilution. Where all
are right together, each is poor together. **The agreement of the many is not
evidence; it is arithmetic, and the arithmetic is division.**

**Ad tertium dicendum quod** the objection errs in supposing that Huw *holds* England.
He does not hold it — **he spends it.** And that which is spent cannot be walled in.
The wall of Argentina is a prison only for him who still stands within it.

### Articulus II — *Utrum certamen tertii loci Finali adiungendum sit?*
**Whether the third-place playoff ought to be joined to the Final.**

**Videtur quod sic.** It seems that it ought.

**Objectio 1.** It is unfitting that a man arrive at the Final with no lawful choice
remaining. For *asinus Buridani*, set between two burned finalists, starves.

**Objectio 2.** More choice is more liberty, and liberty is a good. Therefore four
teams are better than two.

**Objectio 3.** It is proposed by Conrad, who is a man of good will.

**Sed contra,** the game is named **Last Man Standing.** But if no man may be felled,
no man may stand last. Therefore the proposal destroys the thing it proposes to
improve.

**Respondeo dicendum quod** here we must *distinguo*. The *materia* of the game is the
picking of teams; but its *forma* — that which makes it this game and not another —
is **the constraint**. Stranding is not a defect of the game; stranding **is** the
game. It is why the champion is saved, why the finalist is not burned, why Huw's
holding of both France and Spain is a work of art rather than an accident of
bookkeeping. Remove the punishment and every one of those virtues becomes mere
decoration.

And further: under the proposed rule, **40.1% of all decided pots would be won by a
man riding a team from the consolation match.** Thus the pot, whose *telos* is the
champion of the world, would in two cases of five be awarded by a contest which the
players themselves do not honour, and which no model can price, for the men upon the
field do not wish to be there. **We should have replaced skill with a dead rubber,
and called it mercy.**

**Ad primum dicendum quod** Buridan's ass starves not from want of hay, but from want
of a reason to choose. He is a parable against *indifference*, not a petition for
*alms*. The stranded player is not owed a third bale; he is asked why he ate the
first two.

**Ad secundum dicendum quod** liberty granted to all is distinction granted to none.
Splits rise from 77.3% to 80.9%; the spread between the best hand and the worst
compresses by near a quarter. **You have not enlarged the players. You have shrunk
the game.**

**Ad tertium dicendum quod** nothing whatever is alleged against Conrad's will, which
we hold to be entirely good. Yet the Doctors teach that a thing is known *per
effectus*, by its effects. And the effects are these: **Conrad +1.3, Andrea +1.4, and
Huw −2.1 — Huw alone having played well.** The proposal transfers from him who found
the line to those who did not. Of the goodness of the will we say nothing; of the
goodness of the arithmetic, rather less.

**Ad quartum**, which none has raised but all should: it is objected that the whole
disputation may prove idle, should Belgium prevail this evening. **Concedo.**
Providence may spare us the argument entirely — in **20.9%** of worlds *simpliciter*,
and in **80.8%** of worlds should Belgium do their office. Let us therefore dispute
with becoming humility, and watch the football.

*Quod erat demonstrandum.* 🐙
